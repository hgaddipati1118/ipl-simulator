Compiled from extensive web research across ESPN Cricinfo, Olympics.com, Wikipedia, CricTracker, BCCI official documents, and multiple cricket journalism sources.

## 1. Impact Player / Impact Sub Rule (Deep Dive)

### Core Mechanics
- Introduced in **IPL 2023** and continues through the **2025-2027 cycle**
- Each team names **5 substitute players** (not 4) in addition to their playing XI before the match
- During the match, **one** of these 5 may be introduced as the **Impact Player** to replace any player from the starting XI
- The Impact Player can **bat AND bowl** (unlike normal cricket substitutes who can only field)
- The replaced player takes **no further part** in the match -- cannot return, not even as a substitute fielder

### When Can the Substitution Be Made?
- **Before the start of an innings** (before batting/bowling commences)
- **At the fall of a wicket** (replacing the next batter due in)
- **At the end of an over** (between overs)
- **When a batter retires** (voluntary retirement)

### Batting & Bowling Rights
- The Impact Player gets **full participation rights** identical to regular players
- May bat at **any position** in the order
- Can bowl their **full quota of 4 overs**
- Full fielding participation allowed
- If the Impact Player enters after a wicket falls mid-over, they **cannot bowl the remaining deliveries** of that over

### Overseas Player Restriction
- If a team already has **4 overseas players** in the starting XI, the Impact Player **must be an Indian domestic player**
- This maintains the longstanding IPL rule of maximum 4 overseas players at any time

### Match Length Restriction
- Impact Player substitution **cannot be used** if the match is shortened to **less than 10 overs per side**

### Scoring Pattern Changes (Statistics)
- **Average first innings score**: IPL 2022 = 171.1 --> IPL 2023 = 183.4 (12.3 runs higher)
- **200+ totals**: 12.16% of innings in 2022 --> 25% in 2023 --> 28.47% in 2024 --> 36.62% in 2025
- **Runs per over**: Highest pre-impact was 8.64 (2018) --> 8.99 in 2023 --> 9+ in 2024 (first time ever)
- The **9 highest totals in IPL history** were all made after the Impact Player rule was introduced
- Teams regularly crossed 220 and even 250 in IPL 2024
- Impact Players collectively scored **4,500+ runs** and took **320+ wickets** across 2023-2024

### Strategic Implications
- Teams can deploy **batting-heavy lineups** (extra batter as Impact Player if batting collapses)
- Alternatively, bring in a **specialist bowler** when defending a total
- Reduces the value of **all-rounders** (specialists preferred over balanced players)
- MS Dhoni noted: "It's about the mindset -- teams now have the comfort of an extra batter, so they play more aggressively"

### Controversies
- **All-rounder devaluation**: Ricky Ponting argued it "almost negates the role of all-rounders"
- Rohit Sharma expressed concerns about its impact on developing all-rounders for international cricket
- Despite franchise objections, the IPL Governing Council decided to retain it through 2027
- BCCI scrapped the Impact Player rule from other domestic tournaments while keeping it in IPL
- Ethical concerns about tactical batter retirements being exploited

## 2. Powerplay Rules

### T20/IPL Fielding Restrictions

| Phase | Overs | Max Fielders Outside 30-yard Circle |
|-------|-------|-------------------------------------|
| **Powerplay** | 1-6 | **2** |
| **Middle Overs** | 7-15 | **5** |
| **Death Overs** | 16-20 | **5** |

**Note**: In T20 cricket (including IPL), there is only ONE mandatory powerplay (overs 1-6). Unlike ODIs which previously had batting and bowling powerplays, T20s keep it simple.

### Key Details
- During overs 1-6: Only **2 fielders** allowed outside the 30-yard circle
- From over 7 onwards: Maximum **5 fielders** allowed outside the 30-yard circle
- There is **no separate middle overs restriction** in T20s -- the same 5 outside applies from over 7 through over 20
- If the fielding team violates these restrictions (extra fielders outside), the umpire calls a **no-ball** and a **free hit** is awarded

### Historical Changes
- Originally, T20 cricket had different powerplay structures in some formats
- The current simplified format (6 overs mandatory, then 5 outside) has been stable for recent IPL seasons
- In shortened matches, the powerplay overs are reduced proportionally (e.g., 4 overs in a 12-over match)

### For Simulation Purposes
- Overs 1-6: Batting-friendly conditions (only 2 out), expect higher run rates
- Overs 7-20: More defensive field settings available (5 out), run rate typically drops

## 3. DRS (Decision Review System) in IPL

### Reviews Per Innings

| Format | Unsuccessful Reviews Allowed |
|--------|------------------------------|
| **IPL** | **2 per innings per team** |
| Test Matches | 2 per innings per team |
| ODIs & T20Is (international) | 2 per innings per team |

### Technologies Used
1. **Hawk-Eye / Ball-Tracking**: Tracks the ball's trajectory to predict where it would have gone (used for LBW decisions)
2. **UltraEdge (Snickometer)**: Audio-visual technology detecting minimal bat-to-ball contact via sound wave analysis
3. **Hot Spot** (infrared imaging): Detects friction/heat from ball contact -- used in some but not all tournaments
4. **Slow-Motion Replays**: For catches, stumpings, run-outs, boundary decisions
5. **Real-time Snickometer**: Shows sound spikes synchronized with ball passing the bat edge

### Umpire's Call Controversy
- When DRS evidence is **inconclusive**, the on-field umpire's original decision stands -- this is called **"Umpire's Call"**
- If the review results in "Umpire's Call", the **reviewing team retains their review** (no penalty)
- Specifically for LBW: if less than 50% of the ball is hitting the stumps according to ball-tracking, it remains Umpire's Call
- This has been highly controversial -- critics argue it creates an inconsistency where the same ball impact can be out or not-out depending on which team appeals

### Review Retention Rules
- A team **loses a review** only when their challenge is **unsuccessful** (decision unchanged AND not Umpire's Call)
- **Successful reviews** (decision overturned) are retained
- **Umpire's Call** results: review is retained

### IPL-Specific DRS Rules (2023+)
- Players can review **wide ball** decisions using DRS (unique to IPL, introduced in 2023)
- Players can review **no-ball** decisions (height-based no-balls, i.e., above waist on full)
- **Off-side wides** can now be adjudicated using Hawk-Eye technology
- The wide guideline **moves with the batter** -- if a batter moves 1 foot outside off stump, the wide line shifts accordingly

### 15-Second Timer
- Teams have exactly **15 seconds** to signal for DRS after the umpire's decision
- The countdown starts immediately after the umpire's signal
- Cannot challenge if time expires

### Super Over DRS
- Each team gets **1 unsuccessful review** per Super Over

## 4. Strategic Timeout

### Current Rules (IPL 2025)
- **Duration**: **2.5 minutes** per timeout (was increased to 3 minutes for IPL 2022, reports vary on current season -- most sources cite 2.5 minutes for 2025)
- **Number**: **2 timeouts per innings** (one taken by each team)
- Each team gets **one** strategic timeout per innings

### Timing Windows
| Team | Window | Mandatory Fallback |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| **Bowling/Fielding team** | Between overs **6-9** | Auto-called after over 9 |
| **Batting team** | Between overs **13-16** | Auto-called after over 16 |

### How It Works
- The captain or coach can call the timeout during their designated window
- If neither team calls within the window, the umpire **mandatorily initiates** it at the end of the window
- Coaches and analysts rush onto the field with **tablets and data** for tactical discussions

### History of the Rule
- **2009**: Introduced as a **7.5-minute break** at end of 10th over (widely criticized)
- **2010**: Reduced to **5 minutes**, split into two halves during each innings
- **2011-2021**: Duration settled at **2.5 minutes**
- **2022**: Increased to **3 minutes**
- **Current**: Reports vary between 2.5 and 3 minutes for 2025

### Purpose
- Primarily a **commercial break** opportunity (sponsored by CEAT in 2025)
- Also allows teams to discuss strategy, review data, adjust plans
- Test cricket has **no timeouts** (natural breaks exist)

### Controversies
- Sachin Tendulkar and other legends have criticized it for disrupting match flow
- A strategic timeout was controversially called when a team needed just 1 run to win (LSG vs PBKS 2025)
- Critics argue it is purely commercial and slows down the game

## 5. Super Over Rules (IPL 2025)

### When Triggered
- When both teams finish their innings with **identical scores** (tied match)

### How It Works (Step by Step)
1. The team that batted **second** in the main match bats **first** in the Super Over
2. Each team selects **3 batters** and **1 bowler** for the Super Over
3. Each team faces **6 balls** (one over)
4. If a team **loses 2 wickets**, their Super Over innings **ends immediately** (even if balls remain)
5. The team scoring **more runs wins**, regardless of wickets lost
6. Only players from the match (including Concussion Replacements) may participate

### IPL 2025 Specific Time Rules (NEW)
- **First Super Over**: Must start within **10 minutes** of the main match finishing
- **Subsequent Super Overs**: Must start within **5 minutes** of the previous Super Over completing
- **Total time allocation**: **1 hour** from the end of the main match for all Super Overs
- The **Match Referee** informs captains which will be the **final Super Over** if the 1-hour window is about to be breached

### If the Super Over is Also Tied
- **Multiple Super Overs** can be played until a winner is determined (within the 1-hour window)
- **Key restriction**: Batters dismissed in any previous Super Over **cannot bat** in subsequent ones
- **Key restriction**: Bowlers who bowled in a previous Super Over **cannot bowl** in subsequent ones
- If a batter **retires voluntarily** during a Super Over, recorded as "Retired Out" and cannot resume
- The fielding side must **bowl from opposite ends** in subsequent Super Overs

### If Still Tied After 1 Hour
- The match is declared a **tie**
- In league stage: Both teams share points
- In playoffs: The team that finished **higher** in the league standings advances

### DRS in Super Over
- Each team gets **1 unsuccessful Player Review** per Super Over

### Fielding Restrictions
- Same as the **last over** of a normal uninterrupted match (i.e., max 5 fielders outside)

### Ball Selection
- The captain of the fielding team selects the ball from a **box of spare balls**
- The team fielding second may choose to use the **same ball** or pick another from the same box

### Historical Super Overs in IPL
Notable matches include:
- MI vs PBKS (2020) -- Two consecutive Super Overs for the first time in IPL history
- Multiple Super Over finishes across seasons, totaling about 15+ in IPL history

## 6. Free Hit Rules

### When Awarded
A free hit is awarded to the batting team after **any type of no-ball** in limited-overs cricket (including IPL):
- **Front-foot no-ball** (bowler overstepping the crease)
- **Back-foot no-ball** (back foot cutting/beyond return crease)
- **Waist-high full toss** (above waist of batter standing upright)
- **Bouncer above head height** (since 2015 rule change)
- **Fielding restriction violation** (too many fielders outside the circle)
- **Bowler changing sides** without informing umpire
- **Ball bouncing more than twice** before reaching the batter

### Restrictions on the Free Hit Delivery

#### Dismissal Restrictions
The batter can **only be dismissed** in the same ways as a no-ball delivery:
- **Run out** -- YES
- **Hitting the ball twice** -- YES
- **Obstructing the field** -- YES
- **Bowled** -- NO
- **Caught** -- NO
- **LBW** -- NO
- **Stumped** -- NO
- **Hit wicket** -- NO

#### Fielding Position Restrictions
- Fielders **cannot change positions** from where they were for the no-ball delivery, **if the same batter is on strike**
- If the batters crossed (different batter on strike), field changes ARE allowed
- If fielders move illegally, it is another **no-ball**

#### Umpire Signal
- After the normal no-ball signal, the umpire extends one arm straight upwards and moves it in a **circular motion**

### Wide or No-Ball on a Free Hit
- If the free hit delivery is bowled as a **wide**: The free hit carries over to the **next ball** (umpire signals free hit again)
- If the free hit delivery is a **no-ball**: The next ball is **also a free hit** (continues until a legal delivery)
- Runs scored from wides/no-balls on free hits still count normally (extras + any runs scored)

### Key Clarification
- Even if the free hit delivery is called a **wide**, the batter still cannot be dismissed by methods other than run out, hitting the ball twice, or obstruction

## 7. Player Trading Rules

### Trading Window (Between Seasons)
- **Opens**: About one month after the season ends
- **Closes**: Up to one week before the auction date AND continues up to one month before the next season starts
- Only players who were **retained ahead of the auction** can be traded (in non-mega-auction years)

### Types of Trades
1. **Player Swap (Two-way trade)**: Player A from Team 1 swaps with Player B from Team 2
2. **All-Cash Trade (One-way trade)**: A player moves from Team A to Team B, with Team B paying an amount equivalent to the player's auction price

### Rules & Requirements
- **Player consent is mandatory** -- a player must agree to the trade
- If **multiple franchises** express interest, the **selling franchise** makes the final decision
- Overseas player cap (max 8 per squad) must be maintained post-trade
- Trade must comply with salary cap constraints

### Mid-Season Transfer Window
- Introduced in **IPL 2019** (initially only uncapped players)
- From **IPL 2022**: Both capped and uncapped players eligible
- **Maximum 3 players** can be loaned per team per season
- **Eligibility**: Only players who have played in the XI (or as concussion sub) in **fewer than 2 matches**
- The loaned player **cannot play for their parent team** for the remainder of the season
- **Payment**: 50% of loan fee within 7 days of registration, remaining 50% within 7 days of the season's final match

### Can Teams Trade Mid-Season?
- The **mid-season transfer window** allows temporary loans (not permanent trades)
- Permanent trades happen only during the **off-season trading window**

## 8. Uncapped Player Rules

### Definition (IPL 2025)
A capped Indian player becomes **uncapped** if:
1. The player has **not played in the starting XI** in international cricket (Test, ODI, T20I) in the **preceding 5 calendar years**, AND
2. The player **does not have a central contract** with the BCCI

This rule applies **only to Indian players**. Overseas players are always considered capped if they have played international cricket.

### Historical Context
- This rule was originally in place from **2008** but was **scrapped in 2021**
- It was **reintroduced for IPL 2025** -- widely seen as a way to allow CSK to retain MS Dhoni cheaply
- Dhoni last played international cricket in **2019**, making him eligible as uncapped by 2025

### Retention Cost for Uncapped Players
- Every uncapped player costs the team **INR 4 crore** from their purse
- Compare to capped player retention costs: INR 18, 14, 11, 18, 14 crore (for 1st through 5th)

### Quota Requirements
- Maximum **2 uncapped Indian players** can be retained per team
- Maximum **5 capped players** (Indian + overseas combined) can be retained
- Total maximum of **6 retentions** per team (5 capped + 2 uncapped, but 6 total max)

### Players Who Benefited (2025)
- **MS Dhoni** (CSK) -- retained as uncapped at INR 4 crore (instead of 12-18 crore as capped)
- Other retired Indian internationals who met the 5-year criteria

### Controversy
- Former India captain Sunil Gavaskar criticized the rule, noting the uncapped retention price was raised from INR 2 crore to INR 4 crore specifically "to accommodate MS Dhoni"
- Critics argue it creates unfair advantages for teams with retired international stars

## 9. Right to Match (RTM) -- IPL 2025

### How RTM Works
1. Franchises that retained **fewer than 6 players** received **RTM cards** (6 minus number retained = RTM cards)
2. When a player from their 2024 roster comes up at auction, they can **exercise RTM** to match the winning bid
3. **Modified RTM process (2025 NEW)**:
   - The auction proceeds normally until a winning bidder emerges
   - The former team decides whether to use their RTM card
   - If they do, the **highest bidder gets one final chance to raise their offer**
   - If the highest bidder raises, the RTM team must match the **new higher price** or withdraw
   - This gives the auction bidder a "counter-bid" opportunity, making RTM more expensive

### Limits Per Team
- Total of retentions + RTMs = **maximum 6 players**
- If a team retained 5 players, they get **1 RTM card**
- If a team retained 0 players (e.g., PBKS retained only 2 uncapped), they get **4 RTM cards** (up to 6 total)

### Cost Implications
- The **RTM player's salary** counts against the team's purse at the **auction price** (not retention price tiers)
- This can be more or less expensive than a retention slot depending on auction dynamics
- Teams must have sufficient purse remaining to exercise RTM

### Capped vs Uncapped Restrictions
- If a team has already retained **5 capped players**, their remaining RTM can only be used on an **uncapped player**
- If a team has already retained **2 uncapped players**, they **cannot use RTM** on another uncapped player

### RTM Cards Used in 2025 Auction
Notable RTM uses:
- Jake Fraser-McGurk (DC), Rachin Ravindra (CSK), Naman Dhir (MI), Arshdeep Singh (PBKS), Swapnil Singh (RCB), Mukesh Kumar (DC), Sai Kishore (GT), Shamar Joseph (LSG)
